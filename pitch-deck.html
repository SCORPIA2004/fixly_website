The problem

Five channels in. Zero feedback out.

A broken streetlight in Istanbul gets reported through çağrı merkezi, CİMER, Beyaz Masa, social media, and one-off municipal apps — all at once.

Fixly · PitchProduct05

Why it's defensible

The form is easy. Dedup + triage is the moat.

Computer vision

Camera-first: AI fills the form while the citizen looks.

Hugging Face inference proposes one of 11 municipal categories with a confidence score. Async pipeline — submission never waits on the model. Wrong guess? Staff fix it in one click, and that correction is future training data.

Geospatial dedup

50 reports of one pothole = one cluster, not 50 tickets.

PostGIS ST_DWithin matching per category with tunable radius and time window. Reporter counts give staff a built-in severity signal — and citizens see “you and 11 others” instead of silence.

KVKK by architecture: anonymous sessions, inanimate-object detection only (never people or plates), redaction before inference, documented deletion policy.

Hugging Face

async CV inference

  • →DDD bounded context on an open-source Go foundation — multi-tenant from day one, one org for the demo.
  • →Event-driven CV — submit returns instantly; inference and clustering happen off the request path.
  • →Shared TR/EN catalogs (packages/i18n) consumed by both surfaces.
  • →No Kafka, no microservices — in-process bus, one deployable. Hackathon-honest.

Cursor SDK · programmatic

Agent.prompt() script keeps Turkish translations in sync with English — formal siz, municipal terminology, key-parity validated before write.

Guardrails · KVKK

Reproducible AI workflows the team re-runs — not one-off chat sessions.
